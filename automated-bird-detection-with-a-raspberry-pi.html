Automated bird detection with a Raspberry Pi

July 2022

I read about a Raspberry Pi implementation of BirdNET on Hacker News and immediately wanted one for our garden.

BirdNET is a neural net algorithm trained to identity bird species. I worked on a spectacuarly unsuccessful neural network as part of my degree, and so am always keen to find one that actually works.

My first problem was finding a Raspberry Pi - I've never used one before directly, but given their cheap cost and easy availability there was a low barrier to entry... right? Wrong. Worldwide availability is massively constrained across all models; I ordered one online but my retailer gave an updated delivery date of Q1 2023. eBay came to the rescue and I acquired a Zero 2W for a reasonable mark-up.

Installing BirdNET-Pi

BirdNET-Pi is a fantastic implementation of BirdNET for the Raspberry Pi by Patrick McGuire. It wraps the neural net inside an application that records, analyses and presents back the results, all in a nice web UI.
